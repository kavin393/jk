Dinosaurs: The Giants of the Past

Dinosaurs were a diverse group of reptiles that lived during the Mesozoic Era, which spanned approximately 180 million years. These magnificent creatures dominated ecosystems ranging from lush forests to arid deserts.

One of the most famous dinosaurs, the Tyrannosaurus rex, is known for its massive size and formidable teeth. Herbivorous dinosaurs, such as the gentle Brachiosaurus, played a key role in maintaining the balance of their ecosystems. Fossil evidence suggests that modern birds are descendants of certain theropod dinosaurs, showcasing their evolutionary legacy.

Today, paleontologists continue to uncover new species and insights, unraveling the mysteries of life millions of years ago.
